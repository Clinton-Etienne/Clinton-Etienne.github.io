IR-2026-001: Linux Privilege Escalation Investigation

A SOC-style incident report documenting account changes, sudo activity, password modification, and potential privilege escalation activity detected through Wazuh in a controlled home lab environment.

Incident Summary

This investigation focused on Linux security events generated from an Ubuntu target endpoint monitored by Wazuh. The activity included new account creation, sudo activity, password modification, and elevated privilege usage.

Status: Closed

Environment: Wazuh Manager, Ubuntu Server, Kali Linux, VirtualBox

Primary Focus: Privilege Escalation and Persistence

MITRE ATT&CK Mapping

Tactic: Privilege Escalation

Tactic: Persistence

The observed activity could indicate an attacker attempting to gain or maintain elevated access through local account activity and sudo usage.

Investigation Details

Wazuh generated alerts related to Linux account and privilege activity on the monitored Ubuntu endpoint. The events were reviewed to determine whether the activity represented authorized administrative behavior or suspicious activity requiring containment.

Key indicators included account modification, password changes, sudo execution, and activity associated with elevated privileges.

Containment and Remediation

Recommended containment actions included reviewing the affected user account, validating whether the account changes were authorized, restricting unnecessary sudo access, and reviewing authentication logs for additional suspicious activity.

Remediation included enforcing least privilege, reviewing sudoers configuration, improving monitoring for account changes, and documenting all administrative activity.

Lessons Learned

This investigation helped reinforce the importance of monitoring Linux privilege activity, reviewing sudo usage, and documenting account changes. Even in a lab environment, these alerts demonstrate how SIEM tools can help identify behavior that may lead to unauthorized access or persistence.
